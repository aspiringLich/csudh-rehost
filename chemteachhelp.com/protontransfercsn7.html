Proton Transfer Reactions

This page presents exercises in the completion of proton transfer reactions. It is critical that your answers are entered in the format shown below. You should use the "Preview" button to check your answer before submitting it.
Using this page
  • Pressing "New Reaction" will display a partial proton transfer reaction with two species shown and two missing. Enter the missing materials according to the following format:
    Inorganic Acids: HA + B => A- + BH+ Ex: HI + NH3 => I- + NH4+
    Carboxylic Acids(end in CO2H): AH + B => A- + BH+ Ex: CH3CO2H + NH3 => CH3CO2- + NH4+
  • Make certain that the sequence above is followed and you include the charges
  • It is possible that the reaction will presented as the reverse of the general equations shown above and you should structure your response accordingly.
  • You cannot enter subscripts or superscripts in the answer cell, so HNO3 would be entered as HNO3. Also, make certain that the charge, if present is the last entry.
  • Be careful about capitalization.
  • The first time you attempt this page, use the showanswer() and Preview buttons extensively to familiarize yourself with its operation and how to input the values.
  • If you press "Preview" after entering the values, your proposed reaction will be displayed.
  • When you are certain that you have it correct, press "Check Answer" and results will appear in the table.
  • If you miss a problem three times, pressing "Show Answer" will display the complete solution and you will no longer be able submit an answer for that problem.
